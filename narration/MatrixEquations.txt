There are four elementary ray transfer matrices.

The translation matrix for free propagation over distance d has ones on the diagonal and d in the upper right. The height increases by d times theta while the angle is unchanged.

The thin lens matrix has minus one over f in the lower left. The lens changes the angle but not the height of a ray passing through it.

The refraction matrix at a spherical surface accounts for the angle change from Snell's law and the ratio of refractive indices.

The mirror matrix looks identical to the thin lens matrix with minus two over R in the lower left — equivalent to a thin lens with focal length R over 2.
