Bennett Section 1.5.2 discusses linear independence. Two waves are linearly independent if neither can be written as a scalar multiple of the other. Any arbitrary wave in a linear medium can be written as a superposition of the normal modes of that medium. For a uniform one-dimensional medium, the normal modes are the harmonic waves e to the i times kx minus omega t for all values of k. Fourier theory guarantees that any piecewise smooth function can be expressed as a superposition of these harmonic modes.
