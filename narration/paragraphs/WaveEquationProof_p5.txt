This works for any function of x minus vt, not just the quadratic. The chain rule always brings down the same factors regardless of the functional form. For f of x minus vt: partial f over partial x equals f-prime times 1, and partial f over partial t equals f-prime times minus v, where f-prime is the derivative of f with respect to its argument. Taking second derivatives: partial squared f over partial x squared equals f-double-prime, and partial squared f over partial t squared equals v-squared times f-double-prime. So partial squared f over partial x squared divided by partial squared f over partial t squared equals one over v-squared, confirming the wave equation.
