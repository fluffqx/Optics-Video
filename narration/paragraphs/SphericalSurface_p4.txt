At normal incidence, a flat surface has R going to infinity and the equation becomes n-i over s-o plus n-t over s-i equals zero. This gives s-i equals minus n-t over n-i times s-o. For an object at depth s-o in water with n-i equals 1.33, viewed from air with n-t equals 1.00: s-i equals minus 1.00 over 1.33 times s-o equals minus 0.752 s-o. The pool appears 75.2 percent as deep as it actually is — hence the familiar illusion that swimming pools look shallower than they are.
