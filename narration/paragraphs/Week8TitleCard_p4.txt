Fabry-Pérot interferometers routinely achieve resolving powers of millions, far exceeding diffraction gratings. They are used for high-resolution spectroscopy, as optical filters with very narrow passbands, as laser output couplers, and as the reference cavities in frequency-stabilised lasers. The LIGO gravitational wave detector uses Fabry-Pérot cavities to increase the effective path length from 4 kilometres to 1600 kilometres.
