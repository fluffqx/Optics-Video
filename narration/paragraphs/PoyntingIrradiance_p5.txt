For a numerical example: a 5 milliwatt green laser pointer focused to a spot of radius 1 millimetre. The beam area is pi times one millimetre squared, approximately 3.14 times ten to the minus 6 square metres. The irradiance is 5 times ten to the minus 3 watts divided by 3.14 times ten to the minus 6 square metres, giving approximately 1590 watts per square metre. From the irradiance formula, E-naught equals the square root of 2I divided by n epsilon-naught c, which gives approximately 1090 volts per metre. The corresponding peak magnetic field is B-naught equals E-naught over c, approximately 3.6 microtesla.
