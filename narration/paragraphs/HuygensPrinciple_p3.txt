The Fraunhofer approximation — also called the far-field approximation — applies when the screen is far enough from the aperture that the wavefronts arriving at the screen can be treated as plane waves. The condition is L much greater than a squared over lambda, where a is the aperture size and L is the distance to the screen. For a 0.1 millimetre slit, 500 nanometre light, and a screen 2 metres away: a squared over lambda equals ten to the minus eight over five times ten to the minus seven equals 0.02 metres. Since 2 metres is much larger than 0.02 metres, Fraunhofer applies.
