Dispersion in optical fibres limits data transmission rates. A short pulse containing many frequencies broadens as it propagates because each frequency component travels at a slightly different speed. For a single-mode fibre at 1550 nanometres, the dispersion coefficient is about 17 picoseconds per nanometre per kilometre. A pulse with a spectral width of one nanometre broadens by 17 picoseconds per kilometre of propagation. Over 1000 kilometres of undersea cable, this becomes 17 nanoseconds of broadening — limiting the pulse rate to below about 60 gigabits per second for a single channel.
