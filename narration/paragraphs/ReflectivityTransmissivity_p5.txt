For a multilayer optical system like a camera lens with six or eight glass-air interfaces, the reflectivity at each surface compounds: if each surface reflects four percent, then after ten surfaces the transmitted fraction is 0.96 to the power ten, which is approximately 0.665 — only 66.5 percent transmitted. This is why high-quality camera lenses use anti-reflection coatings on every surface: without them, the image would be dim and full of ghost reflections.
