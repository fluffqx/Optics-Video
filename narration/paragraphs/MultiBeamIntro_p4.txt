The key physics: at resonance where delta equals 2 pi times N for integer N, the denominator modulus equals one and the transmitted intensity equals the incident intensity. Every photon gets through, despite the high reflectivity of each mirror. The circulating field inside the cavity builds to a large value — a field enhancement factor of one over 1 minus R — which drives sufficient transmission to account for all the incident power. Off resonance, the denominator can be large, giving very small transmission.
