The modern application: LIGO — the Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory. Two perpendicular arms, each 4 kilometres long, with Fabry-Pérot cavities increasing the effective path length to 1600 kilometres. A gravitational wave from a merging black hole system changes the arm lengths by different amounts, producing a detectable fringe shift. The first detection in September 2015 measured a change in arm length of 10 to the minus 18 metres — one thousandth the diameter of a proton — over both 4-kilometre arms. This is the most precise measurement ever made by humans.
