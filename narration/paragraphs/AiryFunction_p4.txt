The coefficient of finesse F equals 4R divided by one minus R squared should not be confused with the finesse script-F. F appears in the Airy formula. Script-F equals pi root R over one minus R is the ratio of the free spectral range to the full-width-at-half-maximum of each transmission peak. For R equals 0.9: F equals 360, and script-F equals pi times 0.9487 over 0.1 equals 29.8. The peaks are very sharp relative to their spacing.
