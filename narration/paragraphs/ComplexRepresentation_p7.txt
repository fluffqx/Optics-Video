Extending to three dimensions, a plane wave propagating in the direction of the wave vector k-vector is written as A times e to the i times k-vector dot r-vector minus omega t. The dot product k-vector dot r-vector extracts the component of position along the propagation direction. Surfaces of constant phase — wavefronts — are the planes where k-vector dot r-vector equals a constant, which are indeed flat planes perpendicular to k-vector.
