For the sum of two waves: the intensity of psi-one-tilde plus psi-two-tilde equals the modulus squared of the sum, which expands to give modulus-squared psi-one plus modulus-squared psi-two plus 2 times the real part of psi-one-tilde times psi-two-tilde-star. That last cross term is the interference term. It would be much harder to compute starting from sines and cosines.
