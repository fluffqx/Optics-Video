For two lenses separated by distance d, Bennett gives the general formula: one over f-eff equals one over f-one plus one over f-two minus d over f-one f-two. The last term is the correction for separation. For two identical converging lenses each of focal length f separated by d equals f, we get one over f-eff equals 1/f plus 1/f minus f over f squared equals 2/f minus 1/f equals 1/f. The focal length is unchanged — this is the 4-f relay configuration used in Fourier filtering and spatial light modulator systems.
