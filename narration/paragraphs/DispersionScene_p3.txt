The Lorentz oscillator model gives a microscopic understanding of dispersion. Electrons in atoms behave as bound harmonic oscillators with characteristic natural frequencies. When a light wave drives these oscillators, the response depends on how close the driving frequency is to the natural resonance. Well below resonance, the oscillators follow the driving field with a small phase lag, and the refractive index is real and greater than one. Near resonance, the phase lag becomes large, and the index shows a rapid change. Well above resonance, the oscillators cannot keep up with the driving field, and the index approaches one from below.
