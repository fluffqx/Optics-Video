Bennett derives the translation matrix in Section 5.5.1 using the geometry of Figure 5.10. A ray at height y-one and angle theta-one propagates through a medium over horizontal distance d. In the paraxial approximation, tan theta is replaced by theta. The height changes to y-two equals y-one plus d times theta-one, while the angle is unchanged. This gives Bennett Equation 5.35: the translation matrix has 1 and d in the top row, 0 and 1 in the bottom.
