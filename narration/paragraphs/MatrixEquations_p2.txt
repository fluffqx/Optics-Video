The refraction matrix is derived in Bennett Section 5.5.2 from Snell's law at a spherical surface of radius R. The key geometry from Bennett Figure 5.11: the angle phi subtended at the centre of curvature satisfies phi approximately equals y over R in the paraxial limit. Applying paraxial Snell's law n-i times theta-i equals n-t times theta-t and the angle relationships gives Bennett Equation 5.37: the refraction matrix has 1 and 0 in the top row, minus the quantity n-t minus n-i divided by n-t times R in the lower left, and n-i over n-t in the lower right.
