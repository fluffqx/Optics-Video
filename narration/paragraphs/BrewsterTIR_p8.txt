Optical fibres use total internal reflection to guide light. The core has index n-core and the cladding has index n-clad, where n-core is greater than n-clad. Light propagating at a sufficiently shallow angle to the fibre axis hits the core-cladding boundary beyond the critical angle and is totally reflected, bouncing back and forth along the fibre. The numerical aperture NA equals the square root of n-core squared minus n-clad squared, and it specifies the maximum acceptance angle of the fibre.
