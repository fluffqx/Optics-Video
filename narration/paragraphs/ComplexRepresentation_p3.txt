From Euler's formula, cosine theta is the real part of e to the i theta, and sine theta is the imaginary part. This means we can write a wave psi equals A cosine of kx minus omega t as the real part of A times e to the i times kx minus omega t. We define the complex wave psi-tilde as A times e to the i times kx minus omega t. The physical wave is always the real part of this complex quantity.
