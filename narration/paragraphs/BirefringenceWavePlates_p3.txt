A wave plate, also called a retarder, is a slab of birefringent material cut so that the optic axis is parallel to the face of the slab, as shown in Bennett Figure 6.10(c). A beam normally incident on such a slab resolves into two components: one polarised along the optic axis (travelling with index n-e) and one polarised perpendicular to it (travelling with n-o). Since the two components travel at different speeds, one accumulates more phase than the other after passing through the slab.
