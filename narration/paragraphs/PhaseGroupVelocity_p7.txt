Bennett gives a specific example in Section 7.6: a dispersion relation omega equals a k squared, representing a dispersive medium. The phase velocity is v-p equals omega over k equals ak. The group velocity is v-g equals d omega d k equals 2ak equals 2 v-p. The energy envelope travels at twice the crest speed. In water waves, where the dispersion relation for deep water is omega equals the square root of g times k, the group velocity is v-g equals one half times v-p — the envelope moves at half the crest speed. This is directly observable: ripples on a pond appear to travel through the group, emerge at the front, and disappear at the back.
