Consider a concrete example: suppose the dispersion relation is omega equals a k squared, where a is a constant. The phase velocity is omega over k, which equals ak — it depends on k, so this is a dispersive medium. The group velocity is d omega d k, which equals 2ak — twice the phase velocity. The envelope of a wave packet travels twice as fast as the individual crests. You can actually observe this in water waves on a calm lake: ripples spread out and the crests move faster than the overall disturbance.
