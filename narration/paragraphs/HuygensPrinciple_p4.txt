In the Fraunhofer regime, the diffraction integral simplifies to a Fourier transform. The electric field pattern at angle theta is proportional to the Fourier transform of the aperture function, evaluated at spatial frequency u equals sine theta over lambda. This Fourier relationship between aperture and diffraction pattern is one of the most fundamental results in wave optics. It means that a wider aperture gives a narrower diffraction pattern, and vice versa — precisely the wave-optics uncertainty principle.
