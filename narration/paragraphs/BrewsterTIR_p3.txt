The physical explanation is elegant. At Brewster's angle, the reflected ray and the refracted ray are exactly perpendicular to each other: theta-B plus theta-t equals 90 degrees. The p-polarised electric field oscillates in the plane of incidence, which means it oscillates in the direction of the would-be reflected ray. But electromagnetic radiation cannot be emitted along the direction of oscillation of the source dipoles — dipoles do not radiate along their axis. So no p-polarised light can be reflected when the refracted ray is perpendicular to the reflected ray.
