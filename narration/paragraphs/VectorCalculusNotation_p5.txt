The Laplacian, written as del squared or nabla squared, is the divergence of the gradient: del squared phi equals del dot del phi. For a scalar field, del squared phi equals the sum of the three second partial derivatives with respect to x, y, and z. It measures how the value of phi at a point compares to the average of phi in the surrounding neighbourhood. The wave equation del squared psi equals one over v-squared times the second time derivative of psi involves the Laplacian acting on a vector field, where it is applied component by component in Cartesian coordinates.
