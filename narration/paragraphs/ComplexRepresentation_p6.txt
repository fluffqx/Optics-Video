This is also why interference is so interesting: when two waves add, the intensity of the sum is not simply the sum of the intensities. The cross terms produce the interference pattern. In complex notation: the intensity of psi-one plus psi-two equals the modulus squared of psi-one-tilde plus psi-two-tilde, which expands to give the intensities of each wave plus twice the real part of psi-one-tilde times psi-two-tilde-star. That cross term is the interference term.
