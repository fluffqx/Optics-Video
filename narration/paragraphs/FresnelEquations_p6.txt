The energy reflectivity R is the square of the amplitude coefficient: R equals r squared. For our air-to-glass example at normal incidence: R equals 0.04, meaning four percent of the incident energy is reflected. The energy transmissivity T satisfies R plus T equals one, so T equals 0.96 — ninety-six percent transmitted. But notice that T is not t squared — T equals n-t cosine theta-t over n-i cosine theta-i times t squared. The extra factor accounts for the change in beam cross-section at the interface.
