Each optical element is represented by a 2 by 2 Jones matrix. To find the output polarisation state, multiply the Jones matrix by the input Jones vector.

A horizontal polariser passes only the x-component: it has 1 zero zero zero. A vertical polariser has zero zero zero one.

A quarter-wave plate with fast axis horizontal introduces a minus i phase to the y-component: the matrix is 1 zero zero minus i.

A half-wave plate with fast axis horizontal flips the sign of the y-component: 1 zero zero minus 1.

For a sequence of elements, multiply the Jones matrices from right to left — first element on the right — then apply the result to the input vector.
