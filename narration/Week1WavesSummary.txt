Let's recap the three essential equations from Week 1.

First: the wave equation itself — the second spatial derivative equals one over v squared times the second time derivative. This governs all classical wave phenomena.

Second: the harmonic wave relations — v equals f lambda equals omega over k, with k equals 2 pi over lambda and omega equals 2 pi f. Know these relationships cold.

Third: the complex representation — psi tilde equals A e to the i times kx minus omega t. The physical wave is the real part. The intensity is the magnitude squared, which equals A squared.
