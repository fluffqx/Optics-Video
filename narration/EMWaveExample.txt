Given an electric field E pointing in the x-direction and propagating in the z-direction, we need to find the magnetic field B.

The propagation direction is z-hat. Using the relation k-vector cross E equals omega times B, we take k in the z-direction cross E in the x-direction. Z-hat cross x-hat equals y-hat by the right-hand rule. So B points in the y-direction with magnitude E naught over c.

The three vectors — E in x, B in y, and k in z — form a right-handed coordinate system. This is a general property of plane electromagnetic waves.
