The beam expander is a classic and important example. Two lenses with focal lengths f-one and f-two are separated by d equals f-one plus f-two. We calculate the system matrix.

Write the three matrices from right to left: thin lens f-one, free propagation f-one plus f-two, thin lens f-two. Multiply them out step by step.

The result has a C element equal to zero, confirming that the system is afocal. The A element equals minus f-two over f-one, which is the beam magnification. A beam entering with height y and zero angle exits with height minus f-two over f-one times y and zero angle — the beam has been expanded by a factor of f-two over f-one, and the sign reversal means the beam is flipped.

This is the operating principle of a telescope used in reverse, a Galilean beam expander, and a spatial light modulator relay system. The same matrix result applies to all of them.
