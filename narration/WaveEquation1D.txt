Here is the one-dimensional wave equation. Take a moment to look at its structure.

On the left side, we have the second derivative of psi with respect to position x. This is the curvature of the wave in space — how sharply the wave bends at each point.

On the right side, we have one over v squared, times the second derivative of psi with respect to time. This is the acceleration of the wave in time — how quickly the wave is changing at each moment.

The equation is saying: the spatial curvature equals the temporal acceleration, scaled by the wave speed v. Space and time are linked by a single number — the speed of the wave.

Now, what are the solutions to this equation? Any function of the form f of x minus vt is a solution — this represents a wave travelling in the positive x direction at speed v. And any function of the form g of x plus vt travels in the negative x direction.

Notice the key insight: the shape of the wave — whatever shape it has — travels without distortion. The wave just moves.
