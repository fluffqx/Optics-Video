Week 6 is interference — one of the most dramatic demonstrations that light is a wave. When two coherent beams overlap, the result is not simply their sum of intensities. The fields add, and where they add constructively the intensity can be four times that of a single beam. Where they add destructively, the intensity drops to zero.

The key topics are: two-beam interference and the general formula, Young's double-slit experiment and its quantitative analysis, thin film interference and the critical role of reflection phase shifts, fringe visibility and coherence, and the Michelson interferometer.

Interference phenomena underlie a huge range of technology: anti-reflection coatings, laser resonators, optical coherence tomography in medicine, gravitational wave detection in LIGO, and wavelength measurement in spectroscopy.
