The final examination covers all eight weeks. The most important thing to bring is fluency with formulas — you should not need to derive them under time pressure. Here is a rapid review of the key formulas by week.

Week 1: wave equation, harmonic wave relations v equals f lambda, complex representation, superposition principle. Week 2: Snell's law, Fresnel equations for r-s and r-p, R equals r-squared, R plus T equals 1, Brewster's angle tangent theta-B equals n-t over n-i, critical angle sine theta-c equals n-t over n-i, Malus's law I equals I-naught cosine squared theta. Week 3: thin lens equation one over s-o plus one over s-i equals one over f, magnification m equals minus s-i over s-o, lensmaker's equation.

Week 4: translation matrix, thin lens matrix, system matrix and how to extract cardinal points. Week 5: Jones vectors for six standard states, Jones matrices from Bennett Table 6.1, multiplication order M-N times ... times M-1, intensity from modulus-squared. Week 6: interference formula I equals I-one plus I-two plus 2 root I-one I-two cosine delta, Young's fringe spacing delta-y equals lambda L over d, thin film interference conditions, fringe visibility V equals I-max minus I-min over I-max plus I-min.

Week 7: single-slit zeros at b sine theta equals m lambda, Airy disk radius sine theta-one equals 1.22 lambda over D, Rayleigh criterion, grating equation d sine theta-m equals m lambda, resolving power R equals N m. Week 8: Airy function I-t equals I-naught over 1 plus F sine squared delta over 2, coefficient of finesse F equals 4R over 1 minus R squared, finesse script-F equals pi root R over 1 minus R, resolving power equals N times script-F, FSR equals lambda squared over 2nd.

The most common mistakes: confusing F with script-F in the Fabry-Pérot; forgetting the cosine theta factor in thin-film interference for oblique incidence; using the wrong sign in Brewster vs critical angle formula; not using the correct factor for T (it is not simply t-squared). Review the worked examples in Bennett for each of these.
