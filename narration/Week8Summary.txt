Let's collect all the Fabry-Pérot formulas in one place.

The Airy function: I-t equals I-naught divided by one plus F sine squared of delta over 2. This is the transmitted intensity as a function of round-trip phase.

The coefficient of finesse F equals 4R over one minus R squared.

The finesse script-F equals pi root R over one minus R.

The round-trip phase delta equals 4 pi n d cosine theta over lambda-naught.

The order number N equals 2nd over lambda-naught.

The resolving power equals N times the finesse.

The free spectral range in wavelength equals lambda-naught squared over 2nd.

The free spectral range in frequency equals c over 2nd — this is the cavity mode spacing.

And the coherence length of the light source must exceed 2nd for fringes to be visible.

These formulas describe the most powerful spectroscopic instrument based on interference. Study them carefully — the Fabry-Pérot almost always appears on the final exam.
