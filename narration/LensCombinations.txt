When two lenses are used in sequence, the image formed by the first lens becomes the object for the second lens. Apply the thin lens equation sequentially.

For two thin lenses separated by distance d, the effective focal length satisfies: one over f_eff equals one over f_1 plus one over f_2 minus d over f_1 f_2.

When the lenses are in contact — d approaches zero — this simplifies to: the powers add. P_eff equals P_1 plus P_2. This is why opticians measure lens power in dioptres and simply add them.
