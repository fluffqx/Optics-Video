Let's prove that the function psi equals x minus vt, all squared, actually satisfies the wave equation. This is Bennett Example 1.1.

We take the second partial derivative with respect to x, and get 2. Then we take the second partial derivative with respect to t, and get 2v squared. Dividing by v squared gives exactly 2 — matching the left side perfectly. The wave equation is satisfied.

This confirms that any function of the form x minus vt is a valid travelling wave.
